About

India is a country in Asia and more specifically located in South Asia. It is the second most populous country in the world only behind China and is also the seventh largest country in the world. It is widely advised for young adults to visit India as they get to learn about the history and culture. It is a constitutional government that represents a diverse population. It is bordered by 6 countries in the north, west and east. The coastline of India and the south is surrounded by Arabian sea, Bay of Bengal and Indian ocean. The subcontinent of India is set off from the rest of Asia by the Himalayas and adjoining mountain ranges. These mountains fall across India, southern Tibet, Nepal, Bhutan, Pakistan and Afghanistan. Cape Comorin, the southernmost point of India, marks the dividing line between Arabian sea and Bay of Bengal. According to Wolpart S, The north and northwestern India have ethnic affinities with European and Indo-European peoples from southern europe while the south has groups of East African origin. Mark J states, The name of India originates from the Indus river. The civilizations found in India are just as important and rich in history and culture as other major civilizations such as Mesopotamia and Egypt. The indus valley civilization covered more territory than Egypt and Mesopotamia. Many aspects of modern life include inventions and innovations of ancient India such as drainage and sewer systems, mathematics, board games, yoga and meditation. India has a rich and diverse history which would interest any young adult and encourage them to learn more about the ancient times of India. India has a surplus of historical sites which young adults seeking to learn more about India’s history and culture can visit.

India is one of the most ethnically diverse countries in the world and is home to many religions and groups. Being a diverse country, India opens up many opportunities for young adults seeking to learn about the culture and traditions of India. India is a huge country and there are so many regions and cultures to see and explore. India is the birthplace of four religions: Hinduism, Jainism, Buddhism,and Sikhism, with Hinduism being the most popular. India is a diverse multiethnic country that is home to many small ethnic and tribal groups. In terms of languages, India has hundreds of major and minor languages that belong to four different language families: Indo-Iranian, Dravidian, Austroasiatic, and Tibeto-Burman. Majority of Indians speak Indo-Iranian and Dravidian languages. The official languages of India are Hindi and English. India has one of the largest and diversified economies in the world. Some of India’s exports include: Refined Petroleum, Diamonds, Jewellery, Cars, Ready-made garments and leather products. According to Wolpart S, India has three of the most populous and cosmopolitan cities in the world—Mumbai, Kolkata, and Delhi. Three other cities—Bangalore, Chennai , and Hyderabad—are among the world’s fastest-growing high-technology centres, and most of the world’s major information technology and software companies now have offices in India. India is a huge country that includes many natural locations to visit. According to Top 30 Must See Natural Places in India, some of the significant natural locations to visit in India are:- Kerala Backwaters (Kerala), Floating Lake (Manipur), Flower Valley(Uttarakhand), Lonar lake (Maharashtra), Khajjiar Plateau (Himachal),Snow Valley (Kashmir) and Mountain Passes (Ladakh). There are so many more activities and cultures that young adults can participate in to help shape their opinion about India and learn about the culture. These are just some of the natural places and cities young adults can visit to help broaden their view on culture and history of India. These places and activities will help young adults to engage with different groups and cultures and learn more about them.
