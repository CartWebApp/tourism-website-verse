India is a country of many celebrations and traditions and one of the most important ones is Republic Day. This day is celebrated around India by people of all ages, especially young adults. Young adults can participate in various activities to celebrate Republic day. The origin behind this day started when India was finally freed from colonial British rule in August of 1947. The constitution of India was adopted by the government on November 26, 1949 and came into effect on January 26,1950. According to Republic Day 2020, in January 1950, India became the world’s largest democracy with an estimated population of about 359 million people. This completed India's movement towards becoming an independent republic with a democratic government system. Republic day is celebrated on 26 January every year in India. It is significant because it is a day to honour the constitution of independent India. Childrens and young teens hoist the national flag in schools and colleges around India. In the capital, New Delhi, the national flag is hoisted by the president. According to,Republic Day 2020 “ New Delhi hosts the nation's biggest parade. It's quite the spectacle.”. It is also important to know that the parade is conducted by the president and Ministry of Defense. Tourists are encouraged to attend this event because it showcases exhibitions of military equipment, the national flag and armed forces. Other than showcasing its military prowess, this event also promotes India’s diverse culture. Young adults of ages should attend this event as they will get to experience groups of people representing different cultures and religions of India. There are many other activities that young adults can experience and learn about the history of India.

  • Kashmir is a region of the northwestern Indian subcontinent.The region, with a total area of some 85,800 square miles (222,200 square km), has been the subject of dispute between India and Pakistan since the partition of the Indian subcontinent in 1947. The Kashmir region is predominantly mountainous, with deep, narrow valleys and high, barren plateaus. The relatively low-lying Jammu and Punch plains in the southwest are separated by the thickly forested Himalayan foothills and the Pir Panjal Range of the Lesser Himalayas from the larger, more fertile, and more heavily populated Vale of Kashmir to the north. The climate of the region ranges from subtropical in the southwestern lowlands to alpine throughout the high mountain areas. The people in the Jammu area are Muslim in the west and Hindu in the east and speak Hindi, Punjabi, and Dogri.
  • India is home to many caves and one of the most historically rich caves in the world are the Ajanta caves. The destination is widely advised to visit when in India and remains one of the most visited architectural sites in India. Young adults are encouraged to visit this place to learn about the history and ancient architecture. The Ajanta caves consist of more than 30 caves and are known for their Buddhist temple architecture and murals. The caves date from the second century B.C.E to 650 C.E. They are an example of one of India's unique artistic traditions known as rock cut temples. The caves also present paintings depicting the past lives and rebirths of the Buddha, pictorial tales , and rock-cut sculptures of Buddhist deities. Each cave also is filled with sculpture, wall murals, and ceiling paintings. Tourists are encouraged to visit all caves as each cave is unique and they consist of different types of paintings and sculptures. Ajanta caves is a photographer's paradise and young adults will have the opportunity to capture some amazing shots. Young adults are advised to visit this place during the monsoon as the weather is pleasant enough and there is sprawling greenery to capture.
